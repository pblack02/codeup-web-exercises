Yellowstone National Park

  • It was established as the first national park in the United States in 1872.
  • Yellowstone is home to the famous Old Faithful geyser, known for its regular eruptions.
  • The park spans three states: Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho.
  • Yellowstone is renowned for its diverse wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, and herds of bison and elk.

Yosemite National Park

  • Yosemite is located in California's Sierra Nevada mountains.
  • The park is known for its iconic granite cliffs, such as El Capitan and Half Dome.
  • Yosemite Valley features stunning waterfalls, including the famous Yosemite Falls.
  • It is home to a wide variety of plant and animal species, including the giant sequoias.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is located in Arizona and is carved by the Colorado River.
  • It is one of the most popular national parks in the United States, attracting millions of visitors each year.
  • The canyon is about 277 miles long and up to 18 miles wide, and its depth reaches over a mile.
  • The park offers breathtaking viewpoints, hiking trails, and opportunities for rafting in the Colorado River.
